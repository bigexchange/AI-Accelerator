When to Use Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini

All three are good. None of them is best at everything. The reps and operators who get the most out of AI aren't the ones who picked a favorite — they're the ones who know which tool to reach for and why. This guide gives you the decision rules.

What you'll have

The honest answer

All three are good. None of them is best at everything. The reps and operators who get the most out of AI aren't the ones who picked a favorite — they're the ones who know which tool to reach for and why. This guide gives you the decision rules.

The short version: Gemini is already in your Google tools and is best when you don't want to leave them. Claude is best when the work is complex, long, connected to other systems, or needs to be done right. ChatGPT is best for data analysis and building reusable GPTs for your team.

Gemini — use what you already have

Gemini is the easiest win because you don't have to go anywhere. It's already inside Gmail, Google Docs, Google Sheets, Google Slides, and Google Meet. If you're already in a Google tool and the task is straightforward — summarize this, rewrite that, analyze this spreadsheet — Gemini is the fastest path.

Use Gemini when:

You're in Gmail and need a fast summary. A 40-email thread about a contract renewal, a back-and-forth with procurement, a customer escalation chain. Gemini reads it and gives you the TLDR, open items, and action items without you leaving your inbox.

"Summarize my thread with [contact] about [subject]. What decisions were made, what's still open, and what do I owe them?"

You're in Google Docs and need to write or rewrite. The Gemini sidebar is right there. You don't need to copy-paste anything. Ask it to make a section more concise, rewrite a paragraph in active voice, or add a section you're missing — it edits in place.

"Make this section more direct. Lead with the business impact. Cut anything that's filler."

You're in Google Sheets and need formulas or analysis. Ask Gemini what you need in plain English — it writes the formula. Ask it to summarize the dataset — it does. No Excel expertise required.

"Which reps have the highest win rate this quarter? Flag any row where the close date has passed."

You want to summarize a Google Meet recording. Enable transcription before the call. After it ends, ask Gemini to summarize: decisions made, action items by owner, open questions. Then draft the follow-up email. The whole post-call workflow without leaving Google.

You want to use Notebook LM. This is Gemini's best-kept secret and one of the most powerful tools in the whole lineup. Upload any collection of documents — a research report, a set of customer transcripts, a competitive analysis, a product brief — and Notebook LM builds a knowledge base you can chat with. It cites everything. It surfaces connections between documents. It generates an audio briefing you can listen to on the way to a meeting.

BigID use cases for Notebook LM:

  • Upload all your competitive intel documents and ask "what are our three strongest differentiators against Cyera in a financial services deal?"
  • Upload 20 customer call transcripts and ask "what are the most common pain points mentioned across these calls?"
  • Upload the Forrester Wave report and analyst research and ask "what criteria should we be emphasizing in our pitch?"
  • Upload a collection of win/loss interviews and ask "what patterns show up in our losses that don't show up in our wins?"
The Gemini default rule: if you're already in a Google tool and the task takes less than 5 minutes, use Gemini. Don't open a new tab.

Claude — for work that needs to be done right

Claude is where you go when the task is complex, consequential, long-form, or needs to connect to other systems. It's the tool that produces work you'd actually send to a customer or a board member without heavy editing. It's also the tool with the most powerful connection layer — Salesforce, HubSpot, Google Drive, Slack, Gmail, Atlassian, BigID MCP, and more — which means it can pull from and act across your whole stack in one conversation.

Use Claude when:

The output needs to be genuinely good. Not a rough draft you'll rewrite — something you'd send. A proposal. A press release. An executive brief. A competitive analysis. A QBR deck. Claude produces output that sounds like a skilled human wrote it, not like AI generated it. For anything where quality matters, Claude is the first call.

The task is long or complex. Claude handles enormous amounts of context — long documents, long conversations, multi-part tasks. You can paste an entire RFP, a 50-page contract, or a full sales deck and ask Claude to work with all of it at once. Most other tools start losing the thread after a certain length. Claude doesn't.

You need it to connect to your other tools. This is Claude's biggest practical advantage at BigID. With the MCP connectors enabled, Claude can pull a Salesforce account, check your Gmail, look at your calendar, search your Drive, and post to Slack — all in one conversation. No other tool in the stack does this as seamlessly.

"I have a call with [Company] tomorrow. Pull context from Salesforce, check my recent emails with them, and write me a one-page prep brief with talking points."

You want consistent output over time. Claude Projects let you bake in your instructions, voice, and documents permanently. Every conversation in that project starts from the same foundation. If you want your content to always sound like BigID, always follow the same format, always reference the same competitive positioning — build a Project. Gemini and ChatGPT don't have an equivalent that's as useful.

The stakes are high. A customer proposal. A board slide. A press release. Something going to Dimitri. Use Claude. The quality ceiling is higher and the output requires less cleanup.

You're doing research that needs to be cited. Enable web search. Claude will pull live sources, cite them, and flag conflicting information. The output is structured, specific, and sourced — not a confident-sounding hallucination.

You need to build or format a branded document. PDFs, slide decks, formatted reports — Claude's skills at BigID produce properly branded outputs automatically. Gemini and ChatGPT will write the content but won't format it in BigID branding. Claude will.

The Claude default rule: when quality, complexity, or connections to other tools matter — use Claude. When you're building something you'd show a customer or a leader — use Claude.

ChatGPT — for data analysis and team tools

ChatGPT's strongest practical advantages at BigID are two things Claude and Gemini don't do as well: data analysis via code interpreter and building reusable custom GPTs for your team.

Use ChatGPT when:

You have a data file and need instant analysis. Upload a CSV of pipeline data, a spreadsheet of account health scores, a report export from Salesforce — ChatGPT's code interpreter runs Python on it in real time. You get charts, pivot summaries, and analysis without opening Excel, writing a formula, or asking your ops team. It's the fastest path from raw data to insight.

"Analyze this pipeline CSV. Show win rate by segment, top 10 deals by value, and a 6-month trend. Create a chart for each."

You want to build a reusable team tool. Custom GPTs are ChatGPT's version of Claude Projects — but they're more shareable as a standalone link. Build a Sales Enablement GPT with your pitch deck, competitive positioning, and objection handling guide baked in. Share one URL with your whole sales team. They ask it questions like they'd ask a senior rep. No re-prompting, no uploading docs each time.

Good BigID GPTs to build:

  • BigID Sales Coach — pitch positioning, competitive rebuttals, objection handling
  • BigID Content Writer — brand voice, messaging frameworks, campaign briefs
  • BigID Proposal Generator — proposal templates, pricing context, customer-specific framing

You need to run code or test logic. Code interpreter can execute Python, do calculations, test formulas, process files, and generate working scripts. For non-engineers who occasionally need to crunch something programmatically, it's the easiest on-ramp.

The ChatGPT default rule: you have a file you need analyzed or a team tool you want to build and share — use ChatGPT.

The decision tree

Is the task already inside a Google tool (Gmail, Docs, Sheets, Meet)?→ Gemini — it's already there
Do you need to research or chat with a collection of documents?→ Gemini Notebook LM
Do you have a CSV, spreadsheet, or data file to analyze?→ ChatGPT code interpreter
Do you want to build a reusable team tool with one shareable link?→ ChatGPT custom GPTs
Is the output going to a customer, a leader, or into a branded document?→ Claude
Does the task require connecting to Salesforce, HubSpot, Gmail, Slack, or Drive?→ Claude MCP connectors
Is the task long, complex, or consequential?→ Claude
Do you need consistent output across dozens of sessions?→ Claude Projects

What most people get wrong

Using only one tool for everything. ChatGPT users try to make it do everything. Same with Claude. The reps who get the most done use all three for what each one is actually best at.

Not using Gemini in context. Most people open gemini.google.com as a separate tab when Gemini is already sitting in the sidebar of every Google tool they use. The fastest version of Gemini is the one that's already open next to your doc.

Not using Notebook LM. It's genuinely underused at most companies. If you have a collection of documents you find yourself referencing repeatedly — competitive intel, customer research, analyst reports — Notebook LM turns it into something you can actually talk to.

Using Claude for quick tasks that don't need it. If you just need to summarize an email you're already reading in Gmail, use Gemini. Save Claude for the tasks that actually benefit from its depth.

Not building a Claude Project for recurring work. Every time you paste the same brand guide or competitive one-pager into a new Claude conversation, you're doing unnecessary work. Build the Project once. Done.
