The close / push / drop sort

Triage every dealPull all my open opportunities closing this quarter from Salesforce. For each: name, stage, amount, close date, last activity date, MEDDPICC completion %, forecast category, and Deal Next Step. Then give me a verdict for each — Close, Push, or Drop: CLOSE — Stage 4 or 5, recent activity in last 7 days, MEDDPICC above 60%, realistic close date PUSH — Real opportunity but close date is optimistic, Stage 2–3, or activity has slowed DROP — No activity in 30+ days, MEDDPICC below 25%, past close date with no update After the list: total expected close value from Close deals, total pipeline moving to next quarter from Push deals, and total to be closed out from Drop deals.

Daily close week promptPull my close week status from Salesforce. Pro tips

⏰
Run the sweep before your forecast call, not during itThe sweep exists so you walk in knowing your number cold. If you're discovering deal problems in the forecast call, you're running the sweep too late.
📋
Push deals cleanlyA push isn't a loss — but a messy push turns into a lost deal in the first week of the new quarter. Update the date, log the next step, have the conversation.
🏁
The last week is execution, not analysisBy close week the analysis is done. Stop pulling reports and start making calls. The daily close week prompt gives you your one priority each morning.
⚠️
Close out the dropsEvery deal you identify as a Drop should be closed out before the last day of the quarter. Closed lost with a real loss reason is data. An open deal with a past close date is just noise.
