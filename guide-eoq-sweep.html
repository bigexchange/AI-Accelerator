End of Quarter Deal Status Sweep

The end of quarter sweep is a systematic, deal-by-deal review of everything that's supposed to close before the quarter ends. The goal: know exactly which deals are actually closing, which need a push, which should be pushed to next quarter, and which are dead.

🏁
What you'll have
Every deal in the quarter has a verdict: close, push, or drop. Your forecast is clean, your push list managed, and you walk into close week knowing your number cold.

The end of quarter sweep is a systematic, deal-by-deal review of everything that's supposed to close before the quarter ends. You run it in the final three to four weeks of the quarter — ideally on a Monday, before your forecast call, and again in the final week before close. The goal is simple: know exactly which deals are actually closing, which ones need a push, which ones should be pushed to next quarter, and which ones are dead and just haven't been closed out yet.

Done well, the sweep turns quarter-end chaos into a managed close. Done late, it's damage control. Run it now.

What you need before you start

  • Salesforce connected in Claude's tools panel
  • Your quota for the quarter
  • Gmail connected is helpful for pulling thread context on specific deals
  • Set aside 30–45 minutes the first time — subsequent sweeps take less

Step 1: Pull the full quarter picture

Start with everything on the board.

Pull all open opportunities closing this quarter from Salesforce. Give me: total count, total pipeline value, breakdown by forecast category (Commit, Call, Best Case, Omitted) with count and value for each, and breakdown by stage with count and value. Then tell me: what is my weighted pipeline for the quarter using Commit at 85%, Call at 60%, Best Case at 30%? How does that compare to my quota of $[X]? What's my coverage gap if any?

Step 2: The close/push/drop sort

This is the core of the sweep. Every deal closing this quarter gets one of three verdicts: close it (it's happening this quarter), push it (it's real but not this quarter), or drop it (it's not going to close and should be closed out).

Pull all my open opportunities closing this quarter from Salesforce. For each deal give me: name, stage, amount, close date, last activity date, MEDDPICC completion %, forecast category, and Deal Next Step. Then give me a recommended verdict for each deal — Close, Push, or Drop — based on these criteria: Close — Stage 4 or 5, recent activity in the last 7 days, MEDDPICC above 60%, realistic close date Push — Real opportunity but close date is optimistic, Stage 2–3, or activity has slowed Drop — No activity in 30+ days, MEDDPICC below 25%, past close date with no update, or clear signal the deal isn't moving After the verdict list, give me: total expected close value from Close deals, total pipeline moving to next quarter from Push deals, and total to be closed out from Drop deals.

Step 3: The late-stage focus list

Late-stage deals are where the quarter is decided. Pull them separately and go deep.

Pull all my open opportunities this quarter in Stage 4 (Business Negotiation), Stage 5 (Legal Review), Stage 4 Renewal Verbal Yes, Stage 5 Renewal Legal Review, and Stage 3 Renewal Business Negotiations. For each deal give me: name, amount, stage, close date, last activity date, MEDDPICC completion %, forecast category, and Deal Next Step. Then flag any deal that has: a past close date, no activity in the last 7 days, a null amount, or MEDDPICC below 50%. These flags at late stage are the biggest risks in the quarter. Current late-stage flags worth knowing: Right now in the current quarter's late-stage pipeline there are several deals that warrant immediate attention — including a $1.39M deal at 100% MEDDPICC with no logged activity and a past close date sitting in Best Case, a $604K deal in the same situation, and a Fiserv deal in Business Negotiation with 0% MEDDPICC and last activity in November 2024. These aren't just data hygiene issues — they're deals that need an owner decision this week.

Step 4: The stuck deals

Deals that haven't moved in two weeks aren't closing this quarter without intervention. Find them now.

Pull all open opportunities closing this quarter from Salesforce where the last activity date is more than 14 days ago. Sort by amount descending. For each deal give me: name, amount, stage, last activity date, days since last activity, and forecast category. For the top 5 by value, pull any additional context from the Deal Next Step field. These are the deals I need to call or email today — rank them by which one I should prioritize first based on deal size and stage.
Re-engagement prompt for stuck deals I need to re-engage [Deal Name] before end of quarter. The deal has been quiet for [X] days. Pull the Salesforce record — stage, amount, MEDDPICC, Deal Next Step, last activity. Then search for any recent news about [Account Name]. Help me write a re-engagement message that references something current, acknowledges the timing, and creates a reason to talk this week without feeling desperate.

Step 5: The Best Case conversion list

Best Case has $22.6M in it this quarter. Some of those deals will move up to Call or Commit. Most won't. The question is which ones you can actually pull forward.

Pull all my Best Case opportunities closing this quarter from Salesforce. For each deal give me: name, amount, stage, close date, last activity date, and MEDDPICC completion %. Then sort them into two groups: deals where conversion to Call or Commit is realistic this quarter (Stage 3+, MEDDPICC above 60%, recent activity), and deals that are Best Case in name only (Stage 0–2, low MEDDPICC, no recent activity). For the realistic conversion candidates, tell me the one thing I need to do to move each one up this week.

Step 6: Deal-by-deal action plan

For the deals you're closing this quarter, build the specific action plan.

Here are the deals I'm committed to closing this quarter [paste your close list from Step 2]. For each deal, tell me: the most likely obstacle between now and close, the single most important action I should take this week, and whether I need executive involvement, legal acceleration, partner engagement, or something else to get it done. Give me a specific recommended next step for each — not "follow up" but the actual action.
Prompt — executive alignment check Which of my closing deals this quarter have an executive sponsor or economic buyer identified in Salesforce? Which ones don't? For deals closing in the next 30 days without an economic buyer identified, flag them — late-stage deals without an identified decision-maker are at high risk of slipping regardless of stage.

Step 7: The push list

Deals going to next quarter aren't failures — they're next quarter's pipeline. Manage the push well and you start the new quarter with momentum rather than starting from scratch.

Based on the deals I'm pushing to next quarter [paste push list from Step 2], help me: update the close date to a realistic date in Q[next quarter], write a brief Deal Next Step for each that reflects where things actually stand, and identify which ones should be recategorized from their current forecast category. I want to start next quarter with accurate data, not carryover assumptions.
Prompt — push conversation prep I need to have a push conversation with [contact] at [account]. The deal is at [stage] and I don't think it will close this quarter. Help me frame the conversation in a way that maintains momentum and urgency without burning the relationship. What's the right way to set expectations and keep them engaged for a Q[next] close?

The final week sprint

In the last five business days of the quarter, the sweep becomes daily. Here's the prompt to run every morning of close week.

Daily close week prompt Pull my close week status from Salesforce. Give me: deals closed won so far this quarter (total count and value), deals still open with a close date this week (name, stage, amount, last activity), any deal that closed won or lost yesterday, and what I need to close today to hit my number. Tell me my top one priority for today — the single deal most likely to close before Friday if I focus on it.

Reading the end of quarter signals

High Best Case, low Commit — that's the current quarter in a nutshell: $22.6M in Best Case vs. $1.2M in Commit. Either the quarter has a lot of upside that could be pulled in, or the forecast categories haven't been updated to reflect reality. Run the close/push/drop sort immediately.

Late-stage deals with no activity — a deal in Business Negotiation or Legal Review with two weeks of silence is a red flag regardless of MEDDPICC score. Something has stalled. Find out what before it becomes a closed lost in the last week of the quarter.

100% MEDDPICC, past close date, Best Case — this is the most confusing signal in the current pipeline. A deal that's fully qualified but sitting in Best Case with a past close date either has a procurement or legal delay that isn't documented, or the deal isn't as close as the MEDDPICC score suggests. Get on the phone.

Omitted pipeline with late-stage deals — $22.3M is in Omitted this quarter. Some of those deals may be closeable if pulled forward. Check whether any Omitted deals are actually in Stage 4 or 5 — those might be miscategorized, not truly excluded.

Tips

Run the sweep before your forecast call, not during it. The sweep exists so you walk into the call knowing your number cold. If you're discovering deal problems in the forecast call, you're running the sweep too late.

Push deals cleanly. A push isn't a loss but a messy push — wrong close date, no updated Deal Next Step, no conversation with the prospect — turns into a lost deal in the first week of the new quarter. Do the push right: update the date, log the next step, have the conversation.

Close out the drops. Every deal you identify as a Drop should be closed out before the last day of the quarter. Closed lost with a real loss reason is data. An open deal with a past close date is just noise that skews next quarter's starting pipeline.

The last week is execution, not analysis. By the final week of the quarter the analysis is done. Stop pulling reports and start making calls. The daily close week prompt gives you your one priority each morning — focus on that.

Your best close is a customer who already bought. Expansion and upsell deals are faster-moving than new logo deals at quarter end. If you have a late-stage upsell in the pipeline, give it as much attention as your biggest new logo. It closes faster and counts the same.
