Prep for any meeting in 30 seconds

Tell Cowork which meeting you're prepping for. It pulls the calendar event, searches your emails and Drive for related context, and hands you a brief before you walk in.

📋
What you'll have
A one-page meeting brief: who's attending, what was discussed last time, relevant emails and docs, and suggested talking points — in about 30 seconds.

What you'll need

🖥️Claude Desktop
📅Google Calendar
📧Gmail connector
📁Google Drive connector
😩 Without this

Read email → find the date → switch to Calendar → create event → paste title → add time → find the Zoom link → copy it → paste it → add attendees. 3+ minutes minimum.

✨ With Cowork

One prompt → Cowork pulls the event, searches related emails and docs, and hands you a complete brief. Under 30 seconds.

1

The basic prompt — works for any meeting

Just tell Cowork the meeting name or who you're meeting with. It figures out the rest.

Paste into Cowork I have a meeting with [name / company / meeting title] today. Please prep me. Pull the calendar event and get me: 1. Who's attending and their titles/roles if available 2. Any emails in my inbox related to this meeting or person from the last 30 days 3. Any related documents in my Google Drive 4. A 2–3 sentence summary of context — what this meeting is likely about 5. 3 suggested talking points or questions I should come in with Save the brief as "meeting-prep-[name]-[date].md" in my Cowork folder.
2

For recurring meetings — 1:1s, weekly syncs

If you have a standing meeting, Cowork can pull recent history so you're never starting cold.

Recurring meetings I have my weekly [meeting name] in [X hours / this afternoon]. Prep me. Check my calendar for today's event. Then: 1. Search my email and Drive for anything related to this team or project from the last 7 days 2. Look for meeting notes or action items from our previous session 3. Summarize what's likely on the agenda based on recent activity 4. List any open action items I may need to report on 5. Suggest 2–3 things I should bring up Save as "meeting-prep-[meeting name]-[date].md".
3

For external / customer / sales meetings

When you're meeting with a prospect, customer, or external partner — a little extra context goes a long way.

External meetings I'm meeting with [company name / contact name] today. Pull my prep brief. From my Calendar: event details, attendees, any notes in the invite. From my Gmail: all emails with this company or contact from the last 60 days — summarize what's been discussed and any open items. From my Drive: any proposals, decks, or notes related to this account. Also: what stage are we at? What was the last thing we agreed to? Talking points: what should I focus on based on the history? Save as "meeting-prep-[company]-[date].md".
ℹ️
Drive connector noteGoogle Drive connector is only available in private Projects in Cowork. If you don't have it connected, skip the Drive step — Calendar + Gmail alone still give you a solid brief.
4

Auto-run before every external meeting

Use /schedule to have Cowork prep you automatically every morning for any external calls that day.

Scheduled version /schedule every weekday at 8:00am Look at my calendar for today. For each meeting with an external attendee (someone not at my company), create a brief meeting prep note. Pull any related emails from the last 30 days and summarize context, attendees, and 2–3 things I should know walking in. Save all briefs as "prep-[meeting name]-[date].md" in my Cowork folder.

Pro tips

⚡
Make it even fasterSave this as a Cowork Skill — a saved prompt you can trigger with a keyword. Name it something like prep [meeting name] so you can invoke it in two words.
📝
Close the loop after the meetingDrop your notes back into Cowork and ask it to extract action items and draft any follow-up emails. Prep brief and follow-up live together in your folder as a clean record.
⚠️
Thin on brand-new contactsIf there's no email history or Drive docs for someone, the brief will be thin. The value really shows up with recurring contacts and ongoing relationships.
