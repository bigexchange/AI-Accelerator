What you'll have

The Monday morning pipeline brief is a five-minute ritual that sets up your entire week. Before you open email, before your first call, before you look at Slack — you run one prompt sequence in Claude and you know exactly where you stand: what closed last week, what's moving this week, what's at risk, what slipped and wasn't updated, and what your three most important actions are before Friday.

The goal isn't more reporting. It's less anxiety. A rep who starts every Monday knowing their pipeline cold is a rep who's never surprised in a forecast call, never scrambling at quarter end, and never caught off guard by a deal that died quietly.

This guide gives you the exact prompt sequence to run every Monday morning. Save it. Make it a habit. Run it the same way every week.

What you need before you start

  • Salesforce connected in Claude's tools panel
  • Five minutes before your first call of the day
  • A place to paste the output — Slack DM to yourself, a Google Doc, or a running notes file

The Monday morning sequence

Run these five prompts in order. Each one takes about 30 seconds. Together they give you the full picture in under five minutes.

Prompt 1 — Last week's results

Start with what happened. Wins, losses, and net movement.

Pull last week's results from Salesforce. Give me: deals closed won (name, amount, owner), deals closed lost (name, amount, loss reason), new opportunities created (name, stage, amount), and net pipeline change — new pipeline created minus closed lost value. One sentence verdict: was last week net positive or net negative for pipeline?

Prompt 2 — This week's committed deals

What are you calling this week and does it hold up.

Pull all my open opportunities in Commit or Call forecast category closing this quarter from Salesforce. For each deal give me: name, stage, amount, close date, last activity date, and whether a Deal Next Step is logged. Flag any deal where: the close date has already passed, there's been no activity in the last 7 days, or the Deal Next Step hasn't been updated in 7 or more days. These are the deals I need to touch today.

What you're looking at right now: There are currently 25 Commit and Call deals this quarter worth $5.4M. Of the deals across all forecast categories closing this quarter, 34 have had no activity in the last week. Several Commit deals — including Unum, AECOM, Vonage, PenFed, and State Street — have close dates already in the past without being updated. That's your Monday morning to-do list before anything else.

Prompt 3 — Slipped deals that haven't been acknowledged

The quiet killers. Deals that missed their close date and nobody updated the record.

Pull all open opportunities from Salesforce where the close date is in the past and the record hasn't been modified in the last 7 days. For each one give me: name, owner, amount, original close date, and current stage. These deals slipped last week and nobody updated them. Rank by amount descending. What's the total value sitting in unacknowledged slips?

Current state: There are 25 deals worth $8.5M with past close dates that haven't been touched in over a week. That's pipeline that needs to either be re-dated, re-categorized, or closed out before your next forecast call.

Prompt 4 — This week's top three deals to move

Not all deals are equal. These are the ones that deserve your energy this week.

Based on my current open pipeline this quarter, identify the three deals I should prioritize this week. Consider: stage, amount, close date proximity, MEDDPICC completion, and recency of activity. For each deal give me: the name, why it's a priority, and the single most important action I should take on it today to move it forward.

Prompt 5 — The week's one number

Close with the number that matters.

Based on my current Commit and Call pipeline for this quarter, what is my realistic expected close this week? Use Commit at 85% probability and Call at 60% probability. Give me: the expected value, what I need to close to be on track for the quarter, and whether I'm ahead, on track, or behind pace for the quarter given what I've closed so far.

The full brief in one prompt

If you want everything at once — for a morning when you're moving fast — this single prompt pulls the whole picture.

Run my Monday morning pipeline brief from Salesforce. Give me five sections: Last week: Deals closed won, deals closed lost, new pipeline created, net pipeline change. This week's committed pipeline: All Commit and Call deals closing this quarter — name, stage, amount, close date, last activity date. Flag any with past close dates, no activity in 7 days, or no Deal Next Step. Slipped deals: Open deals with a close date in the past that haven't been modified in 7+ days. Count and total value. Top 3 priorities: The three deals I should focus on this week with one action each. The number: Expected close value this week based on Commit at 85% and Call at 60%. Am I on pace for the quarter? Keep it tight — this is a 5-minute brief, not a report.

The shareable version

Some reps and managers share a weekly pipeline brief with their team on Monday morning. Here's how to format it for that.

Prompt — team Slack postTake this week's pipeline brief and format it as a Monday morning Slack message for my team. Lead with last week's wins, then new pipeline, then this week's priorities. Keep it under 8 bullet points. Tone: direct and energizing, not corporate. End with one thing the team should focus on collectively this week.
Prompt — manager updateFormat this week's brief as a short Monday manager update. Three paragraphs: (1) what closed last week and what was created, (2) what I'm committed to closing this week and any risks, (3) what I need from my manager this week if anything. Under 150 words.

Saving it as a reusable prompt

The Monday morning brief is most useful when you run the exact same prompts every week — same structure, same format, same order. Here's how to save it so you never have to think about what to ask.

Option 1 — Claude Projects. Create a Claude Project called "Monday Morning Brief." Paste your prompt sequence into the project instructions. Every Monday, open the project and run it — Claude will have the full context automatically.

Option 2 — Google Doc prompt library. Keep a Google Doc called "Weekly Prompts" with the five Monday morning prompts saved in order. Copy-paste the full block into Claude every Monday. Takes 30 seconds.

Option 3 — Slack reminder. Set a recurring Slack reminder every Monday at 8am: "Run Monday morning pipeline brief in Claude." The reminder opens the habit. The habit opens the week.

Building the weekly habit

The brief is only valuable if you run it consistently. Here's how to make it stick.

Week 1–2: Run the full five-prompt sequence. Get familiar with what each section tells you. Notice which prompt surfaces the most useful information for your specific role.

Week 3–4: Start acting on what the brief surfaces. The top three priorities from Prompt 4 become your first three calls on Monday. The slipped deals from Prompt 3 get updated before noon.

Week 5+: The brief becomes automatic. You know what to expect, you know what to look for, and you start catching problems earlier because you're checking every week instead of discovering them in forecast calls.

Tips

Run it before email, not after. Email is reactive. The pipeline brief is proactive. If you check email first, you'll spend your morning responding to what other people need from you. Run the brief first and spend your morning on what you've decided matters.

Don't skip Prompt 3. The slipped deals prompt is the one most reps skip because it's uncomfortable — those are deals that didn't close when you said they would. That's exactly why it matters. Catching slips on Monday and updating them is dramatically better than having your manager catch them in Thursday's pipeline review.

The top three priorities are commitments, not suggestions. When Claude gives you three deals to focus on this week, treat them as your sales plan for the week. Everything else is secondary unless something breaks.

Forward the brief to yourself. After you run it, copy the output into an email or a Slack DM to yourself with the date in the subject line. After six weeks you'll have a running log of your pipeline movement that's more useful than any Salesforce report.

Compare week over week. After a few weeks, start your Monday brief with: "Last Monday my committed pipeline was $X and my top priorities were [deals]. Here's what actually happened. Now pull this week's brief." The comparison is where the real insight lives.
