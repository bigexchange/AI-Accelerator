Build your morning briefing

Connect Calendar, Gmail, and Slack so Cowork generates a daily digest every morning — today's meetings, relevant emails, and key Slack updates, all in one place before you start your day.

✨
What you'll have
A daily brief — your schedule, priority emails, and Slack highlights — generated automatically and waiting in your Cowork folder each morning.

What you'll need

🖥️Claude Desktop
📅Google Calendar
📧Gmail connector
💬Slack connector
📁A Cowork folder
1

Connect your tools

In Cowork, click the briefcase icon in the sidebar → go to Connectors → add Google Calendar, Gmail, and Slack. Each will ask you to sign in — do all three before continuing.

💡
TipIf the connectors show green checkmarks but don't seem to work, toggle each off, wait 5 seconds, then toggle back on. Known quirk.
2

Run your first briefing manually

Test it before scheduling. Paste this prompt directly into Cowork, see what comes back, and tweak the format to your liking.

Paste into Cowork Good morning! Please create my daily briefing for today. Pull together: 1. CALENDAR — List all meetings today with times, attendees, and any location or video link. 2. EMAIL — Summarize any emails from the last 18 hours that need my attention. Flag anything time-sensitive or requiring a response today. 3. SLACK — Check for any unread messages or mentions from the last 18 hours. Highlight anything that needs a reply or looks urgent. Format as a clean daily brief with clear sections. Save as "morning-brief-[today's date].md" in my Cowork folder.
3

Customize the output

Run it once and see what you get. Common tweaks people make:

  • Add a "Today's priorities" section at the top
  • Filter emails to specific senders or subjects
  • Limit Slack to specific channels — #leadership, #sales-team
  • Flag any meeting with no prep doc or agenda
Customized version Good morning. Create my daily briefing and save as "morning-brief-[date].md". CALENDAR: All meetings today — time, attendees, video link. Flag any over 45 min with no agenda. EMAIL: Unread from the last 24 hours. Group by: (1) Needs response today (2) FYI only (3) Can wait. Skip newsletters. SLACK: Unread mentions and DMs from #leadership, #marketing, #sales-team. Summarize threads — don't list every message. TOP 3: End with a "Top 3 priorities" section based on everything above. Keep it under 5 minutes to read.
4

Schedule it to run automatically

Once you're happy with the output, use Cowork's /schedule command to run it every morning hands-free.

⏰ Using /schedule

Type /schedule at the start of your message, then describe the timing and the task:

/schedule every weekday at 7:30am Run my morning briefing. Pull today's calendar events, unread emails from the last 18 hours, and unread Slack mentions from #leadership, #marketing, and #sales-team. Save as "morning-brief-[date].md" in my Cowork folder.
⚠️
Heads upClaude Desktop must be open and running for scheduled tasks to fire. It won't run if your laptop is closed or the app isn't active.

Pro tips

🧠
Build a brain fileCreate a preferences.md in your Cowork folder that describes your role and how you want things formatted. Reference it in your prompt: "Read preferences.md and format the brief accordingly."
📂
Build a briefs archiveDate-stamped filenames mean you build a searchable log of your days over time. Easy to look back when you need context on when something happened.
🔗
Combine with meeting prepAfter your briefing generates, feed it into the "Prep for any meeting" guide to get a pre-brief for your first call automatically.
