Weekly Pipeline Movement Report

Every Monday morning — or Friday afternoon before you disconnect — you should have a complete picture of what happened to your pipeline in the last seven days. Without it, you're managing by feel. With it, you're managing by fact.

📈
What you'll have
A clean weekly report — new pipeline, wins, losses, stage advances, slipped deals, and net pipeline change — formatted and ready to share.

Every Monday morning — or Friday afternoon before you disconnect — you should have a complete picture of what happened to your pipeline in the last seven days. What came in, what advanced, what closed won, what closed lost, what slipped, and what the net change was versus last week. Without it, you're managing by feel. With it, you're managing by fact.

This guide shows you how to pull that report in one Claude session, slice it any way you need it, and turn it into a format you can share with your manager, your team, or yourself. It takes about five minutes once you have the prompts down. Then you run it the same way every week.

What you need before you start

  • Salesforce connected in Claude's tools panel — this is the only connection required
  • A consistent time to run it — Monday morning or Friday afternoon works best
  • Optionally: a Google Doc or Slack channel to paste the output into each week

The full weekly movement report

This single prompt pulls the complete picture. Run it every Monday to start the week knowing exactly where you stand.

Pull my weekly pipeline movement report from Salesforce for the past 7 days. Give me six sections: - New pipeline created — all opportunities created this week, with name, owner, stage, amount, and close date - Closed won — deals closed won this week with name, amount, and owner - Closed lost — deals closed lost this week with name, amount, owner, and loss reason - Stage advances — deals that moved forward at least one stage this week, with name, old stage, new stage, and amount - Slipped deals — open deals whose close date passed this week without closing, with name, amount, stage, and current close date - Net pipeline change — total new pipeline created minus closed lost minus slipped, with a one-sentence summary of whether the week was net positive or negative Format it cleanly so I can paste it into a Slack message or a Google Doc.

Section by section — if you want more control

Sometimes you want to pull just one section, go deeper on a specific area, or share different pieces with different people. Here's how to pull each section independently.

New pipeline createdPull all opportunities created in Salesforce in the last 7 days. For each one give me: opportunity name, owner, stage, amount, close date, lead source, and primary use case. Sort by amount descending. Then give me: total count, total pipeline value, and a breakdown by stage.
Closed won this weekPull all opportunities closed won in Salesforce in the last 7 days. For each one give me: opportunity name, owner, amount, close date, primary use case, and win reason if logged. Give me a total won count and total won value. If there were no wins this week, tell me that clearly.
Closed lost this weekPull all opportunities closed lost in Salesforce in the last 7 days. For each one give me: opportunity name, owner, amount, primary loss reason, and competitor if logged. Give me a total lost count and total lost value. Group by primary loss reason — I want to see if there's a pattern.
Stage advancesPull all open opportunities in Salesforce that changed stage in the last 7 days. For each one give me: opportunity name, owner, current stage, amount, and close date. Sort by amount descending. Flag any deal that skipped a stage — that's worth a closer look.
Slipped dealsPull all open opportunities in Salesforce where the close date is in the past and the record hasn't been updated in the last 7 days. For each one give me: opportunity name, owner, amount, original close date, and current stage. These are slips that haven't been acknowledged — flag them and suggest whether each should be updated, re-categorized, or closed out.

Cuts that actually tell you something

The basic report tells you what happened. These follow-up prompts tell you what it means.

Net pipeline change — the number that mattersBased on this week's numbers: total new pipeline created was $X, total closed lost was $Y, total slipped was $Z. What was my net pipeline change this week? Is my pipeline growing, holding, or shrinking? If it's shrinking, what do I need to create next week to get back to neutral?
Where is new pipeline coming fromOf the new opportunities created this week, break them down by: lead source, primary use case, persona, and industry. Are there any patterns in where new pipeline is coming from? What's the highest-converting source this week?
Loss pattern analysisLook at the closed lost deals from the last 4 weeks. Group by primary loss reason and primary competitor. What are the top three reasons we're losing? Is any one competitor showing up disproportionately? What does that suggest about where we need to sharpen our pitch or our qualification?
Rep-level breakdownBreak down this week's pipeline movement by rep. For each rep on the team give me: new pipeline created, deals advanced, deals closed won, deals closed lost, and net pipeline change. Flag any rep who had no new pipeline created and no stage advances this week.
Coverage checkBased on my current open pipeline and this week's movement, do I have enough pipeline to hit my number for the quarter? Assume a standard close rate of [X]% and tell me: what my current pipeline coverage ratio is, whether I need to create more pipeline this week, and how much new pipeline I need to stay on track.

The shareable version

Once you've pulled the report, format it for sharing.

Prompt — Slack summaryTake the weekly pipeline report above and write a short Slack message I can post to my team channel. Keep it to 6–8 bullet points. Lead with the wins, then new pipeline, then losses and slips, then the net change. Tone: direct and factual, not cheerleader-y.
Prompt — Manager updateTake the weekly pipeline report and write a 3-paragraph manager update. Paragraph 1: what closed and what was created. Paragraph 2: what moved forward and what slipped. Paragraph 3: what I'm focused on next week and any risks I want to flag. Keep it under 200 words.
Prompt — Save to DriveFormat this week's pipeline report as a clean document and save it to my Google Drive in the Sales Reports folder as "Pipeline Movement Report — Week of [date]."

Making it a habit

The report is most useful when you run it consistently and compare week over week. Here's how to build the comparison in.

Week over week comparisonI ran this same report last week. Here were last week's numbers: new pipeline $X, closed won $Y, closed lost $Z, net change $W. Compare to this week's numbers. Is the trend improving or declining? What changed most significantly week over week? What does the trajectory look like for the rest of the quarter?
Month-end rollupPull a month-end pipeline summary for [month]. Give me: total new pipeline created, total closed won, total closed lost, net pipeline change for the month, average deal size, and top five deals that advanced or closed. Format it as a one-page monthly summary I can include in a board or leadership update.

The Monday morning routine

Here's the exact sequence to run every Monday. Takes five minutes, sets up your whole week.

Step 1 — Run the full reportPull my weekly pipeline movement report from Salesforce for the past 7 days. [Full prompt from above.]
Step 2 — Check the slipsOf the slipped deals in this report, which ones are still worth pursuing this week? Pull any recent news on each account and tell me the one thing I should do today to re-engage.
Step 3 — Prioritize the advancesOf the deals that advanced stages this week, which are closest to closing and what do I need to do to keep them moving? Pull the Salesforce record for each and give me a recommended next action.
Step 4 — Identify this week's top threeBased on everything in this report — stage, amount, close date, recent activity, and MEDDPICC — what are the three deals I should prioritize this week? Give me one specific action for each.

Tips

Run it at the same time every week. The value compounds when you run it consistently. A one-time snapshot is useful. A twelve-week trend is how you see patterns before your manager does.

Don't skip the loss reasons. Closed lost data with good loss reasons logged is the most actionable data in Salesforce. If your reps are logging "Other" or leaving it blank, the report loses half its value. Use the analysis prompts to catch this and fix it.

The slipped deals section is the most important one. Wins and losses are history. Slipped deals are where you still have a chance to act. Every slipped deal with no close date update is a deal that's probably dying quietly — catch it early.

Save your prompts. Once you've tuned the weekly report prompt to show exactly what you want, save it somewhere you can copy-paste it every Monday. Claude doesn't need a new prompt every week — the same one works every time.

Share it before anyone asks. Sending your manager a weekly pipeline summary before they ask for it is a different kind of rep than one who waits to be asked. The report does the work. You get the credit.
