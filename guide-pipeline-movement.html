Weekly Pipeline Movement Report

What came in, advanced, closed won, closed lost, and slipped — every Monday in five minutes. The full picture of what happened to your pipeline in the last seven days.

📈
What you'll have
A clean weekly report: new pipeline, wins, losses, stage advances, slipped deals, and a net pipeline change — formatted and ready to share with your manager or team.

What you'll need

🔗Salesforce connector
1

The full weekly movement report

Full weekly reportPull my weekly pipeline movement report from Salesforce for the past 7 days. Give me six sections: 1. NEW PIPELINE CREATED — all opportunities created this week with name, owner, stage, amount, and close date 2. CLOSED WON — deals closed won this week with name, amount, and owner 3. CLOSED LOST — deals closed lost this week with name, amount, owner, and loss reason 4. STAGE ADVANCES — deals that moved forward at least one stage this week with name, old stage, new stage, and amount 5. SLIPPED DEALS — open deals whose close date passed this week without closing, with name, amount, stage, and current close date 6. NET PIPELINE CHANGE — total new pipeline created minus closed lost minus slipped, with a one-sentence summary Format it cleanly so I can paste it into Slack or a Google Doc.
2

Pull individual sections

New pipeline onlyPull all opportunities created in Salesforce in the last 7 days. For each: opportunity name, owner, stage, amount, close date, and lead source. Sort by amount descending. Then: total count, total pipeline value, and breakdown by stage.
Slipped dealsPull all open opportunities in Salesforce where the close date is in the past and the record hasn't been updated in the last 7 days. For each: opportunity name, owner, amount, original close date, and current stage. Flag them and suggest whether each should be updated, re-categorized, or closed out.
Loss pattern analysisLook at the closed lost deals from the last 4 weeks. Group by primary loss reason and primary competitor. What are the top three reasons we're losing? Is any one competitor showing up disproportionately? What does the pattern suggest about where to sharpen the pitch?
3

The shareable version

Slack summaryTake the weekly pipeline report above and write a short Slack message I can post to my team channel. Keep it to 6–8 bullet points. Lead with the wins, then new pipeline, then losses and slips, then net change. Tone: direct and factual, not cheerleader-y.
Manager updateTake the weekly pipeline report and write a 3-paragraph manager update. Paragraph 1: what closed and what was created. Paragraph 2: what moved forward and what slipped. Paragraph 3: what I'm focused on next week and any risks to flag. Keep it under 200 words.
4

The Monday morning routine

⏰
Five-minute Monday sequenceRun these four prompts in order every Monday before you open email.
Step 1 — Last week's resultsPull last week's results from Salesforce: deals closed won, deals closed lost, new opportunities created, and net pipeline change. One sentence verdict: was last week net positive or negative?
Step 2 — This week's committed dealsPull all my Commit and Call deals closing this quarter from Salesforce. Flag any with past close dates, no activity in 7 days, or no Deal Next Step.
Step 3 — Top 3 prioritiesBased on my current open pipeline this quarter, what are the three deals I should prioritize this week? Give me one specific action for each.

Pro tips

📅
Run it at the same time every weekThe value compounds when you run it consistently. A twelve-week trend is how you see patterns before your manager does.
🔍
The slipped deals section is the most importantWins and losses are history. Slipped deals are where you still have a chance. Every slipped deal with no close date update is probably dying quietly — catch it early.
📤
Share it before anyone asksSending your manager a weekly pipeline summary before they ask is a different kind of rep than one who waits. The report does the work. You get the credit.
