Build a Branded Slide Deck with the PPTX Skill

Generate a fully branded BigID .pptx file from a prompt — correct fonts, colors, logo placement, and layouts, all automatic. Open it in Google Slides or PowerPoint and it's ready to present.

📊
What you'll have
A downloadable .pptx with BigID's gradient, Montserrat headlines, correct logo placement, and your content already in it — without touching PowerPoint until it's ready.

What every deck includes automatically

Colors
Purple → pink → orange gradient, charcoal darks, off-white lights
Fonts
Montserrat headlines, Open Sans body text
Logo
White horizontal on dark slides, gray stacked icon on light slides
Footer
Gradient bar flush to the bottom of every slide
Output
.pptx — opens in Google Slides or PowerPoint
⚠️
LibreOffice noteLibreOffice renders gradient text differently. Always verify final output in Google Slides or PowerPoint.
1

Trigger the skill — just ask

You don't need to mention the skill by name. Any of these work:

Build a 12-slide BigID sales deck for an enterprise call with a financial services CISO. Goal: get them to agree to a POC. Cover: the data security problem, why legacy tools fail, BigID's approach, key use cases, a customer spotlight, and a clear next step.
I have a presentation that needs BigID branding. Here's the content slide by slide [paste]. Format it correctly — don't change the content, just apply the right layout and brand.
2

Know your slide types

Reference these names in your prompt and Claude picks the right layout automatically.

Layout nameBest for
Cover slideTitle + subtitle, dark background — always slide 1
Stat card grid4 metrics or numbers with pink outline cards
Left-bar list3–4 items with colored left-bar labels
Customer spotlightLogo, challenge/result bullets, outcome stats
Use case grid (3×2)Six use cases with brand cell
Use case grid (4-column)Four use cases in white outline cards
ROI / Proven at ScaleHero metric + breakdown stat cards
Roadmap / timelineQuarterly or phase-based innovation roadmap
Section dividerDark slide to break between major sections
Using layout names Build a 10-slide BigID deck: Slide 1: dark cover — "AI Runs on BigID" Slides 2–3: left-bar list for the problem and our approach Slide 4: stat card grid with four key metrics Slides 5–6: use case grid (3×2) with 6 use cases Slide 7: customer spotlight Slide 8: ROI / Proven at Scale Slide 9: roadmap Slide 10: dark closing slide with next steps
3

Give Claude your actual content

Real stats and real titles always beat generated content for a presentation that's going anywhere real.

Stats + customer spotlight On the ROI slide: 70% reduction in data discovery time, 94% policy automation rate, 3.2x faster audit response, $2.4M average annual value. Put $2.4M as the hero stat. Customer spotlight — global bank: Challenge: couldn't see what data AI models were training on. Solution: classified 2.3B records across 400+ sources. Outcome 1: reduced sensitive data exposure by 68%. Outcome 2: passed SOC 2 in 6 weeks instead of 6 months.
4

Iterate on what Claude builds

Content change On slide 4, change the headline to [new headline] and replace the third bullet with [new bullet].
Layout change Slide 6 has too much text for a stat card layout. Convert it to a left-bar list instead with 4 items.

Pro tips

🗂️
Ask for the structure firstBefore generating slides, ask Claude to suggest a structure for your audience and goal. Review it, edit it, then ask Claude to build it. Easier to adjust an outline than a built deck.
💾
Save your promptIf you build a deck structure you like, save the prompt. Reuse it for the next deck and just swap the content — the brand and structure are already dialed in.
