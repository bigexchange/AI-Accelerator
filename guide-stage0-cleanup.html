Stage 0 Cleanup for My Region

Stage 0 — Qualification — is where deals go to get real or get gone. It's the top of the funnel, and it's also where pipeline goes to quietly rot if nobody's paying attention.

🧹
What you'll have
A clean Stage 0: every deal triaged, Deal Next Steps logged on everything you're keeping, dead weight closed out with real loss reasons, and a number you can defend.

Stage 0 — Qualification — is where deals go to get real or get gone. It's the top of the funnel, and it's also where pipeline goes to quietly rot if nobody's paying attention. Across the org there are hundreds of open Stage 0 opportunities at any given time — and the majority have no Deal Next Step logged, no recent activity, and in some cases close dates that have already passed. That's not a pipeline. That's a wish list.

This guide walks you through using Claude and Salesforce MCP to do a proper Stage 0 cleanup: surface what's real, flag what's stale, and turn a messy qualification stage into an honest, workable pipeline.

What you need before you start

  • Salesforce connected in Claude's tools panel
  • Optionally: Gmail connected to pull email thread context on specific accounts

What a healthy Stage 0 deal looks like

Before you clean, know what you're cleaning toward. A Stage 0 deal that deserves to stay in your pipeline should have:

  • A logged Deal Next Step — a specific action with a date, in the Deal Next Step field
  • Recent activity — a call, email, or meeting in the last 30 days
  • A realistic close date — not in the past, not three years out
  • An amount — even a rough estimate
  • A clear qualification path — you know why this account could buy and what it would take to get there

If a deal is missing most of these, it needs to either get worked or get closed out.

Step 1: Pull your Stage 0 pipeline

Start with a complete picture of everything sitting in Qualification in your territory.

Pull all my open Stage 0 Qualification opportunities from Salesforce. For each one give me: deal name, amount, close date, last activity date, and whether a Deal Next Step is logged. Then sort them into three buckets: Active (activity in the last 30 days), Stale (no activity in 30+ days), and Dead (no activity ever logged or close date in the past).
If you want to look across your team or region Pull all open Stage 0 Qualification opportunities from Salesforce for my region/territory. Group by owner and give me a count and total pipeline value per rep. Flag any rep with a high number of deals sitting in Stage 0 with no recent activity or no Deal Next Step logged.

Step 2: Triage deal by deal

For each deal in your Stage 0, run a quick triage. You're making one of three decisions: advance it, re-engage it, or close it out.

Quick triage prompt Pull the Salesforce record for [opportunity name]. Tell me: last activity date, Deal Next Step and when it was last updated, close date, amount, MEDDPICC completion percentage, whether a champion and economic buyer are identified, and any notes. Based on this, give me a recommendation — should I advance this deal, schedule a re-engagement, or close it out? If re-engage, suggest how. If close out, suggest the right loss reason.
Bulk triage — review stale deals fast Pull all my Stage 0 opportunities with no activity in the last 30 days. For each one give me the deal name, amount, days since last activity, MEDDPICC completion %, and a one-line recommendation: advance, re-engage, or close out. Be direct.

Step 3: Re-engage the stale ones worth saving

Some deals went quiet because life happened — not because the opportunity died. Here's how to wake them up.

Re-engagement prompt I need to re-engage [Account Name]. The opportunity has been in Stage 0 with no activity for [X days]. Pull what Salesforce has on this account — including the persona, primary use case, current vendor, and compelling event if logged. Then search for any recent news — funding, executive changes, AI initiatives, data security incidents, or regulatory news — that gives me a reason to reach back out. Write a short re-engagement email that references something current and reopens the conversation naturally.

Step 4: Close out the ones that aren't real

A closed-lost deal you can learn from. An opportunity sitting in Stage 0 with no activity and a 2028 close date is noise that inflates your pipeline and wastes your time during every review. BigID has specific loss reasons in Salesforce — use the right one so the data is useful.

Close-out prompt I need to close out [opportunity name] from Salesforce. Based on the record, suggest the most appropriate loss reason from BigID's standard list — options include No Budget/Lost Funding, Non Responsive, No Decision, Project 12+ Months Out, 0 Stage — No Timeline, 0 Stage — No Need, 0 Stage — Expired, and others. Write a brief close loss note I can log before closing it out.
Bulk close-out Here are the Stage 0 deals I've decided to close out: [list]. For each one, suggest the right BigID loss reason and a one-line close loss note I can log. Keep it factual and clean.

Step 5: Advance the ones that are real

For deals with genuine momentum or clear potential, get them out of Stage 0 and into Discovery properly.

Advance prompt The [opportunity name] deal is ready to move from Stage 0 to Stage 1 Discovery. Pull the current Salesforce record and help me: write a Deal Next Step with a specific date, draft a short activity note summarizing where we are, confirm whether the persona and primary use case are logged, suggest a realistic close date based on deal size and complexity, and flag any MEDDPICC fields that should be filled in before this deal goes into the next pipeline review.

Step 6: Log Deal Next Steps on everything that stays

The single biggest thing you can do to make your Stage 0 pipeline credible: log a Deal Next Step on every deal you're keeping. The correct field in BigID's Salesforce is Deal Next Step — not the standard Next Step field — and there's a companion Deal Next Step Updated Date field that tells your manager exactly when you last touched it.

Bulk next-step prompt Here are the Stage 0 deals I'm keeping in my pipeline: [list]. For each one I'll give you a brief status. Help me write a specific, dated Deal Next Step I can log in Salesforce. The next step should be concrete — not "follow up" but "send POV proposal by [date]" or "schedule discovery call with CISO by [date]."

The cleanup summary view

After you've triaged, re-engaged, closed out, and advanced — pull a clean summary of where things stand.

Pull my current Stage 0 pipeline from Salesforce. Give me a clean summary: total number of deals, total pipeline value, how many have a Deal Next Step logged, how many have activity in the last 30 days, and how many have a realistic close date. This is what I'll bring to my manager.

Run this monthly

Stage 0 accumulates noise faster than any other stage. The reps with the cleanest pipelines run a Stage 0 scrub at the start of every month — before the forecast call, not during it.

Monthly cleanup prompt Pull all my Stage 0 Qualification opportunities from Salesforce. I want to do my monthly cleanup. Give me: the full list sorted by last activity date, a count of deals with no activity in 30+ days, a count with no Deal Next Step logged, and a count with a close date in the past. Then walk me through the list and help me make a decision on each one — advance, re-engage, or close out.

The four questions for every Stage 0 deal

Before you decide what to do with any deal, ask these four. Claude can help you answer all of them from Salesforce and web research combined:

1. Is there a real person we've spoken to? If there's no logged activity and no contact, it's not a deal — it's an idea. Close it out or find the right person and start a real conversation.

2. Has anything changed at the account? A deal that went cold six months ago might be worth re-engaging if the company just hired a new CISO, announced an AI initiative, or disclosed a breach. Search before you close anything out.

3. Is there a plausible path to a decision? You need a buyer, a budget cycle, and a reason to act now. If the Compelling Event field is blank and you can't fill it in, the deal isn't qualified.

4. What's the next concrete step? Not "reach out." Not "follow up." A specific action with a specific person by a specific date, logged in the Deal Next Step field. If you can't name it, the deal stays stale.

Tips

Don't inflate your pipeline to look busy. A Stage 0 with a lot of deals and no activity isn't a pipeline — it's a liability. Every stale deal is one your manager will ask about, one that skews your forecast, and one you'll have to explain. Clean it out and own a smaller, tighter, more accurate number.

Close-lost is not failure. Closing out a dead deal is good data hygiene. Log a real loss reason — BigID's list is specific enough to be useful — and move on.

Use the MEDDPICC field as a quick quality check. A Stage 0 deal with 0% MEDDPICC completion and no activity is almost certainly not real. A deal with 40%+ and recent activity is worth a closer look before you close it out.

Use the re-engagement window. Deals that went quiet in the last 60–90 days are often still recoverable — especially if something has changed at the account. Deals that have been untouched for 6+ months with no context need a much stronger reason to stay in your pipeline.
