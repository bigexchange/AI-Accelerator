What you'll have

A territory health snapshot is a complete picture of one region — pipeline distribution, stage mix, activity levels, closed won, ICP coverage, whitespace, and competitive exposure — pulled in one session and formatted so you can act on it or present it. It's the report you should be running before every QBR, every leadership update, and the start of every quarter.

Without this view, you're managing a territory by instinct. With it, you can see exactly where momentum is building, where deals are going quiet, and where the gaps are before they become problems.

What you need before you start

  • Salesforce connected in Claude's tools panel
  • The area or territory name you want to analyze — BigID's areas in Salesforce are East, West, Midwest, EMEA, APJ, Federal, LATAM
  • Your quota for the territory if you want coverage analysis included
  • Optionally: web search enabled for market context on specific accounts

How BigID's territory structure works in Salesforce

Opportunities in BigID's Salesforce are organized by two geographic fields:

  • GeoNew__c — top-level: North America, EMEAAPJ, Federal, LATAM
  • AreaNew__c — more granular: East, West, Midwest, EMEA, APJ, Federal, LATAM

Use AreaNew__c for territory-level analysis. It gives you the right cut for a rep or RVP reviewing a specific region.

The full territory health snapshot

This single prompt gives you the complete picture for any territory. Swap in your area name and quota.

Pull a complete territory health snapshot for the [Area] territory from Salesforce. Give me eight sections: - Pipeline summary — total open deals, total pipeline value, and breakdown by stage (count and value at each stage) - Closed won this year — total won deals, total won value, win rate vs. total closed, and top 5 wins by amount - Closed lost this year — total lost deals, total lost value, top 3 loss reasons by frequency - Coverage ratio — weighted pipeline vs. quota of $[X], using close rate assumptions of 15% for Stage 0–1, 30% for Stage 2, 50% for Stage 3, 70% for Stage 4, 85% for Stage 5 - Activity health — how many open deals have had no activity in the last 30 days, no Deal Next Step logged, or a close date already in the past - Rep breakdown — pipeline value and deal count per rep in the territory, flagging anyone below 2x coverage - Competitive exposure — breakdown of open deals by primary competitor logged - Snapshot verdict — a one-paragraph summary of the territory's overall health: what's strong, what's at risk, and the one thing I should focus on this week Format it cleanly so I can paste it directly into a QBR deck or a leadership update.

Section by section — for deeper cuts

Pull any section independently when you need more detail.

Pipeline distributionPull all open opportunities in the [Area] territory from Salesforce. Break down by stage — show count and total pipeline value at each stage. Then tell me: is the stage mix healthy? Is there enough late-stage pipeline to support this quarter's close, and enough early-stage pipeline to feed next quarter? Flag any stage that looks disproportionately heavy or thin.
Closed won analysisPull all closed won opportunities in the [Area] territory from Salesforce this year. Give me: total won count and value, average deal size, win rate, top 5 wins by amount with owner names, breakdown by primary use case, and breakdown by persona. Where is this territory winning? What's the average sales cycle on closed won deals?
Activity health checkPull all open opportunities in the [Area] territory from Salesforce. Flag every deal that has: no activity in the last 30 days, no Deal Next Step logged, a close date already in the past, or a MEDDPICC score below 25%. Give me counts for each flag and list the highest-value deals that are flagged. These are the territory's biggest hygiene risks right now.
Rep-level breakdownPull open pipeline by rep for the [Area] territory from Salesforce. For each rep give me: total pipeline value, deal count, stage distribution, number of deals with no activity in 30 days, and number of deals with no Deal Next Step. Flag any rep who looks thin on pipeline, thin on activity, or both. I want to know where the coaching priorities are.

Quarter-start territory review

Run this at the beginning of every quarter to set the right baseline before you start forecasting.

I'm starting a new quarter for the [Area] territory. Pull a complete baseline from Salesforce: total open pipeline coming into the quarter broken down by stage, deals that carried over from last quarter without closing (count and value), last quarter's closed won and closed lost totals, win rate last quarter, and current coverage ratio for this quarter's quota of $[X]. Then tell me: what do I need to do in the first two weeks of the quarter to set up a strong close?

Reading the territory signals

Large Renewal Untouched pool — this is deferred revenue that needs active management. Renewal Untouched deals that aren't being worked are churn risk. Pull them, assign owners, and start renewal motions early.

High stale deal count — EMEA's 80 stale deals and East's 43 are the most visible examples in the current data. Stale deals either need to be re-engaged or closed out. A high stale count usually means reps are carrying too many deals to actively work, or qualification standards were too loose at the top of the funnel.

Federal pipeline inflated by placeholders — Federal's $1B+ number is almost entirely a single Stage 0 placeholder. Always strip out outlier amounts before drawing conclusions about Federal pipeline health.

West thin on closed won relative to pipeline — West has $86M in open pipeline but only $1.9M closed won this year. That's a conversion problem, not a pipeline problem. The analysis question is whether it's a stage mix issue, a qualification issue, or a deal size mismatch.

East carrying the number — East is responsible for the majority of BigID's closed won this year. That's concentration risk at the org level and worth flagging in any leadership discussion about territory balance.

Tips

Run it before every leadership conversation. Walking into a QBR, a pipeline review, or a board prep without a current territory snapshot is like showing up to a customer call without doing your research. Five minutes with Claude before any leadership meeting puts you in control of the narrative.

Separate vanity pipeline from real pipeline. Total pipeline looks impressive. Weighted pipeline tells you what you can actually close. Always lead with weighted coverage, and flag inflated outliers before presenting numbers upward.

The stale deal count is your territory's pulse. More than any other single metric, the number of deals with no activity in the last 30 days tells you whether a territory is being actively managed or whether reps are carrying wish lists. Check it every week.

Renewal health is territory health. BigID has significant renewal pipeline across every territory. Renewals that slip to Churn don't just hurt ARR — they hurt the win rate, the coverage ratio, and the rep's credibility. Include renewal health in every territory snapshot.
